200 mile bike ride!


On Saturday, March 20 2021 I completed 200 mile bike ride. In all, it was great day & I enjoyed the ride. See the Strava post here.

the route

the why

I did it for a couple reasons. Mainly:

  • I like doing physical challenges just to see if I can do them - like running all the famous hills in SF or biking 112 miles around the 0.75 mile Golden Gate Park cycing track and more
  • My friend Morgan did a 400km ride & that was inspiring … so I figured if he can do 400km, I can at least do 200mi!
  • The ironman bike leg is 112 miles so maybe if I did 200 miles, 112 miles would feel short. I did something similar by running a trail ultramarathon before my first marathon in 2019.
  • I just felt like biking. Sometimes I think to myself, “I just want to bike all day” … so I tried it! It’s nice being outside, taking it all in, seeing the scenes, & letting your mind wander.

some thoughts

This was a nice route! Here’s are some thoughts -

I got off to an early start in the dark. I always like running & riding in the dark before the world wakes up. Cañada road north & Ocean Beach SF were beautiful.


my bike ... in the dark

I was trying to keep my pacing conservative, but I found myself in a 2 man paceline near Sam Taylor park north & couldn’t let my new friend down! Whatever excess energy I spent there was worth it, since it was a great ~8 mile stretch of lively, net-downhill, 25-30 mph riding.

In Tomales, I stopped at a 7-11 for refreshments & made a birthday phone call. When I I got back on my bike, I had a flat tire. I changed it & got another flat – I hadn’t checked the tire & there was a metal shard in it. Since I blew two tubes, I needed more. I went to 2 bike shops that were closed, but the 3rd shop had the tubes I needed. This was the nadir of ride – I was 83 miles in, a bit tired, & had just spent close to an hour dealing with tubes & re-supply. It was between 12pm & 1pm and I was worried about making back before dark (2 of my 3 lights had died). Luckily though, the stop was an opportunity to rest & I felt good when I got back to it ... I eventually got home while there was JUST enough light to get by.

Logistically, this weekend worked well for me to do this ride so I wanted to seize the opportunity. But, I had broken the derailleur, derailleur hanger, chain, & rear wheel on my standard road bike the week before … so I did the ride on my time trial bike. I like my time trial bike & am fine w/ the aggressive positioning. It likely helped too in some of the windy sections!


road bike ... not ready for the double century

The wind! Usually it’s the case that I blame headwinds from slowing me down & take all the credit for tailwinds. HOWEVER … today was different. The stretch from Petaluma to Tomales had a ripping headwind – BUT the next 100 miles from Tomales to the SF peninsula had a tailwind helping me out.


in Tomales ... see the wind on the water ... how many snickers in am i?

I don’t REALLY think that hard about fueling & nutrition. On this ride I ate EIGHT snickers bars, 2 banannas, a bag of chips, 2 gels, & an apple. Sometimes water & sometimes gatorade. I meant to stop for proper food, but was somewhat worried about the time and was loving the snickers and that’s just what I wanted and was feeling good.

Based on my prior riding I guessed that 180 W was probably a safe power output that I could hold for ~12 hours. I want to race the Ironman at 230-250 W so I figured I would slash a bit off that. In terms of “perceived effort,” I thought to myself that if any point my effort felt harder than a challenging, all-day hike, I was riding too hard. Using a power meter helped me show restraint in the early miles.

Doing efforts like this solo makes it easy to go at your own pace, stop when you feel like it, ride when you feel like it, & deviate from the plan. I was worried I would not be able to complete the ride & did not want any social pressure to get myself injured & didn’t want to rope anyone into logistically supporting my personal challenges. That said, could definitely be fun to do with friends next time!


I made some rounding errors & was going to be short on hitting 200 miles by the time I returned home … so did 15 miles of laps on the Golden Gate Park cycle track. This was fun since I was able to pick up my power late into the ride (miles ~150 - ~170) and hit these 15 miles at 22.2mph. This was encouraging for my Ironman since this was good velocity on tired legs.


a nice 15 mile pick-up from 155m-170mi ... and picking up the speed helped me get home by dark!

Overall, pacing was the biggest thing that made this ride enjoyable. I’ve had 60 or 80 or 100 mile rides that were FAR worse than this. Here, I started convervatively, ended feeling pretty good, and got it done. I’ve been doing 1 100 mile every month from January to June in preparation for my June Ironman & have been getting better at these types of long effort.

what’s next

Next, I’m focused on race-specific training for an Ironman June 27th & will save other big personal endurance challenges for after that. I will likely try to do an Everest ride in July or August.

After this ride, I came across this GCN video about STAGE 1 1903 Tour de France … it was 467km / 290mi! I had no idea how different the race was then. The guys in the GCN video ride it on 1903-style equipment & it looks like a blast. It would be fun to do something wild like that with a friend.

I mentioned my very broken road bike – I’ve decided to disassemble every part & paint it. We’ll see how it goes. Excited for my new old bike!
